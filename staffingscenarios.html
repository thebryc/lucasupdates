Role Descriptions — Scenario 1 Learning Team
📅 0 teaching nights · Full time: strategic leadership, staff development, program systems
This is Chasity's year to lead at full scale. She has spent years earning the knowledge, relationships, and judgment this role demands — and this year, with a fully staffed team and zero teaching nights, she finally has the time and space to apply it. The work ahead isn't new territory for her; it's the work she's been doing in the margins. Now it gets the center.

The Director of Programs sets direction, develops people, builds the systems that make great programming repeatable, and holds the standard for what a BRYC Fellow's experience should be. Chasity is ready for all of it.

Note on title level: Whether the role evolves toward Chief of Programs is a question leadership will evaluate together — based on scope, organizational need, and Chasity's continued growth in the dimensions below.
Set Strategic Direction
  • Define and communicate the vision for BRYC's 8th–11th grade program, ensuring alignment across staff, curriculum, and Fellow experience
  • Lead the separation of 9th and 10th grade into fully distinct cohorts with grade-appropriate curricula
  • Drive formalization of Fellow data systems — interest, academic, and postsecondary data captured early, tracked longitudinally, used to inform support; partner with Elin on design and implementation
  • Define explicit Fellow expectations and communicate them clearly to Fellows, guardians, and staff
  • Partner with Aareena on curriculum alignment and the rollout of ACT prep into 10th grade in Spring 2027
Develop and Manage Learning Staff
  • Directly manages Lauren, Kendrick, New FT Hire, and Brei (upon return)
  • Conducts structured 1-1 check-ins focused on OKRAs, facilitation quality, and Fellow engagement
  • Leads facilitation walkthroughs and peer observations across both campuses — not to evaluate, but to develop
  • Coaches with specificity: behavior-level facilitation feedback, not just general encouragement
  • Builds a consistent professional development rhythm for the team — not one-off sessions, but a culture of getting better
Own Scheduling, Logistics, and Operations
  • Leads campus and night assignments based on enrollment data, transportation needs, and staff availability
  • Partners with Marcus on logistics — transportation, nutrition, physical space, van coordination
  • Coordinates with Forrest on enrollment tracking and cohort-building timelines
  • Owns the program calendar — fall retreat, end-of-year Symposium, Saturday programming
  • Oversee matriculation of incoming 8th–11th grade Fellows — when Fellows are handed off from RAM, ensure no drop in communication or warmth; work with Angela to ensure every new Fellow feels connected from day one
  • Organize and plan fall orientation in partnership with Angela
  • Over the summer, finalize what asynchronous participation looks like for extended Fellows — how they still engage virtually (virtual mentors, digital programming, etc.) when they cannot attend in person
  • Maintains a backup facilitation coverage plan; fills in herself only when no other option exists
Build and Codify the Program Model
  • Drives codification of BRYC's full program model — curriculum, facilitation standards, and Fellow support systems documented and transferable; partner with Elin on curriculum components
  • Partners with Kendrick to ensure career community programs (Legal Fellows and beyond) scale beyond any one person
  • Partners with Angela on the mentor program — recruitment, assignment, training, retention
  • Partners with Jakia to ensure smooth 11th-to-12th grade transitions
  • Participates in cross-functional leadership conversations; brings program perspective to org-wide decisions
Growth Areas & Opportunities
Strengths to Build On
  • ✓ Strategic thinker who designs systems and analyzes data effectively
  • ✓ Connects naturally with Fellows; manages team with high autonomy
  • ✓ Creative problem-solver — builds systems that scale (FYT, BRYC store)
  • ✓ Receptive to feedback; eager for strategic thought partnership
  • ✓ Relational superpower: uses joy and humor to build Fellow connection and make BRYC fun
Growth Edges for 2026–2027
  • → Organizational visibility: Don't wait to be asked. Weekly updates, visible initiatives, and strategic thought leadership surfaced unprompted are the markers of a leader ready for expanded scope.
  • → Accountability beyond the direct team: Holding peers and other PMs to electric programming and IFS standards — even when it's uncomfortable — is the work that separates directors from organizational leaders. Build that muscle.
  • → Attendance as an organizational metric: Own the data, own the trends, own the interventions — not just for the learning team but across all programs. This is the non-negotiable leadership indicator.
  • → Sustained relational investment: Strong in the moment; the opportunity is consistency. Visible presence in Fellows' lives beyond program walls — games, events, social media, celebrations — is what IFS looks like at the director level.
  • → Advocacy and presence: Good work in the background doesn't earn expanded leadership. Speak up, challenge assumptions, bring solutions. Make the impact visible.
The path to Chief of Programs runs through this year. The question isn't whether Chasity is capable — it's whether she makes that capability visible and extends it beyond her own team. That's the work.

📅 1 teaching night · ACT curriculum ownership · Saturday ACT prep series
Aareena owns the quality and coherence of ACT instruction across BRYC's out-of-school program. With ACT prep expanding into 10th grade for the first time in Spring 2027, her role is to build a curriculum sequence that carries Fellows from their first exposure in 10th grade through their official ACT in 11th grade — and to ensure every facilitator who touches ACT content (Lauren, New FT Hire, Kirsten, and any subs) is equipped to deliver it well. She also leads BRYC's Saturday ACT Prep series, which generates revenue from the public while giving BRYC Fellows free access. Aareena operates as a peer to Chasity — with regular check-ins and shared ownership of the 10th–11th grade experience — rather than reporting up through the learning team.
Own ACT Curriculum Across 10th–11th Grade
  • Develop and maintain a cohesive ACT curriculum sequence for 10th and 11th grade, ensuring intentional skill progression year over year; partner with Elin on scope and sequence design
  • Lead the introduction of ACT prep into 10th grade beginning Spring 2027 — establishing this as Fellows' baseline entering score for 11th grade
  • Establish instructional consistency across all ACT cohorts; align with Lauren, New FT Hire, Kirsten, and any substitutes on content, pacing, and delivery standards
  • Explore partnerships (e.g., MasteryPrep) and resources that could strengthen BRYC's ACT programming
  • Target 17+ as the TOPS floor; BRYC's 21.6 average as the north star
Build and Support ACT Facilitators
  • Ensure Lauren, New FT Hire, Kirsten, and subs are equipped to teach ACT content — not just familiar with it
  • Develop a facilitator preparation protocol: here's the curriculum, here's how to internalize it, here's what good ACT facilitation looks like at BRYC
  • Serve as first-call backup when any ACT facilitator is unavailable
  • Facilitate 1 ACT night per week as a standing commitment, keeping her grounded in the Fellow experience
Lead Saturday ACT Prep Series
  • Design and facilitate 2–3 Saturday ACT Prep series per year (fall, spring, summer) at BRYC's Downtown campus
  • Public sessions are open to the Baton Rouge community on a paid basis; BRYC Fellows attend at no cost
  • Determine session format, frequency, and pricing in partnership with leadership
  • Revenue from public sessions goes directly to BRYC
  • Recruit Fellows who would most benefit from Saturday sessions ahead of official ACT dates
  • Other learning staff can teach Saturday sessions for additional pay
Generate ACT Performance Reports
  • Produce semesterly ACT performance reports covering trends, cohort-level results, and year-over-year comparisons — for internal team use, families, district leadership, and funders
  • Generate on-demand reports when requested by leadership, partners, or grant compliance requirements
  • Go beyond data extraction: include trend analysis, interpretation, and real-time intervention recommendations so reports drive decisions, not just documentation
  • Translate ACT data into accessible language for non-technical audiences (families, board members, funders) without losing analytical rigor
Peer Lead Responsibilities (with Chasity)
  • Maintains a regular check-in rhythm with Chasity given their shared ownership of 10th–11th grade Fellow experience
  • Collaborates on scheduling, cohort assignments, and pacing decisions that affect both ACT and SRL programming
  • Escalates HR or management items to Josh (COS); escalates curriculum or program matters to Elin (COP)
Growth Areas & Opportunities
Strengths to Build On
  • ✓Operationally excellent — strong at execution, follow-through, and managing autonomously
  • ✓Deep knowledge of what helps Fellows advance on the ACT; results-oriented with genuine commitment to Fellow success
  • ✓Receptive to feedback and coachable
  • ✓Strong relational connector — builds authentic relationships with Fellows and partners
Growth Edges for 2026–2027
  • →Translating intelligence into strategic leadership: Knowing what works in the room is not the same as building a true pedagogical scope and sequence. The Director of ACT role requires building a coherent, transferable curriculum framework — not just a collection of effective practices.
  • →Executive-level communication: Written and verbal communication — emails to partners, reports to funders, proposals to district leadership — must reflect organizational perspective, not just programmatic detail. Polish and precision matter at this level.
  • →Interpersonal calibration in high-stakes settings: Directness and authenticity are assets. The growth edge is reading rooms, catching unspoken cues, and adapting approach when finesse is required — especially in partnership development and external meetings.
  • →Strategic planning rigor: Shifting from executing plans to driving planning — pre-morteming partner meetings, analyzing what could go wrong, bringing strategic recommendations rather than status updates.
The intelligence is there. The growth question for this year is whether Aareena can translate what she knows into a curriculum that works without her in the room, and communicate it in ways that build BRYC's external credibility.

📅 3 teaching nights/week · 8th grade YPAR · Career community programs
Kendrick owns BRYC's 8th grade experience from end to end — curriculum, facilitation, and the career-connected community ecosystem that makes YPAR real for Fellows. Beyond the classroom, he's BRYC's point person for launching and sustaining programs like Legal Fellows: structured, career-interest communities that connect Fellows to local practitioners and real-world application of their research. The goal isn't one program — it's a replicable model. Legal Fellows is the first proof of concept. Health Fellows, juvenile justice, financial literacy, or whatever emerges next will follow the same framework. Kendrick also provides facilitation coaching to new learning staff — not as a subject matter expert in every curriculum area, but as someone who knows what good BRYC facilitation looks and feels like.
Facilitate 8th Grade YPAR
  • Teach 8th grade cohort 3 nights/week, targeting 50 Fellows across two cohorts (~25 each)
  • Lead Fellows through the full YPAR arc: youth voice → community mapping → issue selection → stakeholder engagement → action
  • Connect research topics to local practitioners as speakers, shadowing hosts, and community partners
  • Facilitate the end-of-year Symposium capstone
  • Maintain Fellow engagement through individualized outreach on attendance, grades, and mentor relationships
Launch and Sustain Career Community Programs
  • Serve as BRYC's point person for Legal Fellows (Year 1) — coordinate four Saturday sessions with the District Attorney's Office, support Fellows in leading the capstone community forum
  • Build a replicable framework so future career communities (Health Fellows, financial literacy, juvenile justice, etc.) can be stood up with minimal additional overhead
  • Identify community partners — business owners, nonprofit leaders, legal and healthcare professionals — who can anchor future communities
  • In partnership with Chasity, shape the broader "communities" framework across all grades
  • Recruit Fellows into career communities; manage applications and logistics
Instructional Support for Learning Team
  • Provide facilitation coaching to new learning staff — call-and-response, Fellow engagement, pacing, energy management, classroom culture
  • Conduct periodic observations of other cohorts with structured feedback
  • Lead monthly facilitation development sessions for the full learning team
  • Model lesson internalization: review → anticipate misconceptions → scaffold → deliver
Codify YPAR Curriculum
  • Complete full codification of YPAR curriculum into a transferable, unit-based format by end of summer; partner with Elin on curriculum structure and standards
  • Ensure all materials are "Kendrick-agnostic" — any qualified facilitator can pick them up
  • Incorporate current cultural references; avoid generic filler content
Fellow & Family Engagement (IFS)
  • Maintain weekly outreach rhythm: guardian memos, absent Fellow texts, CA follow-up
  • Support fall retreat and end-of-year Symposium planning
Growth Areas & Opportunities
Strengths to Build On
  • ✓Exceptional facilitator with a distinct, effective style that Fellows respond to
  • ✓Genuine vision for connecting YPAR to real community work — not just content delivery
  • ✓Strong community relationships and instinct for the right partners
  • ✓Natural coach — already thinking about how to develop new staff, not just his own cohort
Growth Edges for 2026–2027
  • →Codification and transferability: The YPAR curriculum works because Kendrick runs it. The growth edge is making it work without him — fully documented, unit-based, and internalized by someone who has never seen him teach.
  • →Scaling the community program model: Legal Fellows is year one of a framework, not a one-off. The test is whether a second community (Health Fellows, financial literacy, juvenile justice) launches using the same structure with minimal additional design from Kendrick.
  • →Instructional coaching breadth: Coaching facilitation culture and call-and-response is a strength. The next step is developing the muscle to coach across curriculum areas he hasn't personally facilitated — with humility and specificity, not just general feedback.
